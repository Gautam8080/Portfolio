Interactive Decision Tree

Center for Collegiate Recovery Communities ‚Äì TTU (May 2019 ‚Äì Aug 2019)

This study proposes a supervised, controlled approach to the CART algorithm used in pairing orphaned children to foster parents. The new system lets users decide or alter the question at specific nodes. The proposed research intends to achieve its designated functions through an Interactive GUI that will dictate a live feature selection at each node. The user will be given the choice of features in order of most to least information gain, something the user can overrule the algorithm. Once the appropriate questions have been introduced to the model at intended nodes, the user can let the model proceed in its original form to complete the tree.

The research also draws a comparison between separate Decision Trees applied vs. not applied to K-means clustered data. A significant accuracy is obtained with improved performance and a better insight into the data.
